A village gram panchayat is a local self-government institution in India that is responsible for the administration of a village or group of villages. It is a democratic organization that is elected by the people of the village to govern the village and take decisions on matters related to development, welfare, and maintenance of the village. The gram panchayat is headed by a sarpanch who is elected by the members of the panchayat. The members of the gram panchayat are elected every five years through a democratic process. The gram panchayat is responsible for providing basic amenities like water supply, sanitation, healthcare, and education to the residents of the village. They also have the responsibility to implement various government schemes and programs related to rural development. The gram panchayat is an important institution for rural governance and development in India.
